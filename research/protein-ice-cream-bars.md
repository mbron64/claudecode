# Protein Ice Cream Bars — Deep Research Report

**Concept:** A startup selling protein-fortified frozen dessert _bars_ (stick novelties) in two
launch SKUs — **Strawberry Shortcake** and **Chocolate Crunch** — aimed at the fitness / "gains"
audience.

**Method:** Six parallel research agents (market, competitors, formulation, regulatory,
manufacturing/unit-economics, go-to-market), each returning sourced findings; the highest-stakes
claim (the "20g-bar whitespace") was cross-verified against two independent agents. Source-quality
flags are carried through. Date: 2026-07-01.

> **Reliability note:** Many primary sites (eCFR, FDA.gov, trade press, some market-research firms)
> block automated fetch, so a number of figures come from search-result excerpts of those same
> sources rather than full-text reads. Figures corroborated across ≥2 independent sources are marked
> high/medium confidence; single SEO-firm numbers are flagged low and should be re-verified before
> use in a deck or fundraise.

## TL;DR recommendation

1. **Positioning — own the number nobody in the bar format owns yet.** Every current protein _stick
   bar_ tops out at ~15g (Orka, California Ice, Nick's); only _pints_ reach 20g. Launch at **20g
   protein per bar** with low/no added sugar. This out-specs every bar competitor and is defensible
   _because it is hard to formulate_ — the whitespace and the moat are the same fact.
2. **Formulation — dairy, casein-forward, allulose-sweetened.** Use a **milk-protein-isolate / whey
   blend** (native ~80:20 casein:whey) so you can hit 20g without the "wheying"/chalky off-note that
   kicks in above ~8–10% whey. Build creaminess with **micellar casein/MPC + inulin** to minimize
   gums; sweeten with **allulose + monk fruit** for scoopability and a near-zero added-sugar panel.
3. **Differentiate the flavors, don't copy them.** California Ice already sells a **15g Strawberry
   Shortcake** bar — beat it on protein (20g) and brand, or you're a me-too. **"Chocolate Crunch" is
   genuinely open** as a named 15–20g protein-bar SKU; lead with it.
4. **Manufacture via a co-packer; do not build a line.** Extruded/coated stick bars with inclusions
   are a specialized co-pack capability — shortlist is small (Perry's, Brothers Desserts, Ice Cream
   Factory). Expect MOQs of tens of thousands of units and real freeze-thaw R&D.
5. **Labeling — you're a "frozen dairy dessert," and any protein claim triggers PDCAAS.** A low-fat/
   high-protein bar almost never meets the 10% milkfat "ice cream" standard, so call it a _frozen
   dairy dessert_. Critically, the moment you put "20g protein" on front-of-pack, FDA requires a
   **PDCAAS-corrected** %DV — dairy protein clears it easily; this is the exact trap behind the
   Beyond Meat lawsuits.
6. **Go to market natural-channel-first (Sprouts), fitness-influencer-led, TikTok-fronted.** Frozen
   DTC is a margin sink ($40–$100+/shipment) — use it for data/gifting, not volume. Budget slotting
   and free-fill as a core launch cost, not an afterthought.

## 3. Formulation — 20g/bar is aggressive; casein + allulose is the path

**The core physics:** raising protein trades away exactly what buyers want. In controlled studies,
higher protein drove **overrun 94.9%→33.9%, hardness 13.6→47.7 N, melt rate 0.24→0.74 g/mL** (Current
Research in Nutrition & Food Science, 2023). A ~3 oz (≈80g) bar at 20g protein is **~25% protein by
weight** — well above the **~8–10% whey ceiling** where a "wheying"/chalky off-note and sodden
texture appear. That is _why_ 20g bars don't exist yet, and why the answer is casein-forward, not
whey-forward.

**Recommended stack:**

- **Protein:** milk-protein-isolate (native ~80:20 casein:whey) or a whey/casein blend as the base;
  add **micellar casein / MPC** to carry viscosity + water-binding and cut ice-crystal growth. Peer-
  reviewed optimum for low-fat: ~5–6% micellar casein, ~8.7–9.7% total protein (Applied Food
  Biotech, 2021). Dairy beats plant here — pea/soy give more melt resistance but grittier texture and
  beany off-notes (JDS/PMC, 2024). Reserve plant for a separate vegan SKU only.
- **Sweetener/scoopability:** **allulose** is the workhorse — ~0.7× as sweet as sugar but **~1.9× the
  freezing-point-depression power**, so it keeps a low-sugar bar from freezing rock-hard; top up
  sweetness with **monk fruit/stevia** (no bulk, no FPD). Cap **erythritol ≤8%** (it crystallizes and
  makes bars _harder_/gritty above that). Allulose + erythritol are synergistic (Cargill, 2024).
- **Creaminess without a long additive list:** **inulin** binds water, adds creaminess, replaces some
  sugar/fat, and earns a "prebiotic fiber" claim. Use casein/MPC + inulin to **minimize gums**; add
  only a minimal stabilizer touch (locust bean gum ± low carrageenan) if freeze-thaw shows iciness.
- **Coating & crunch (the bar-specific risk):** enrobe after demold in a **compound coating** (cocoa
  - veg/coconut oil — temper-free, sets fast on a cold bar). The #1 failure is **moisture migration**
    softening cereal/cookie crunch and cracking the coat. Fixes: a **fat/lipid barrier layer** between
    ice cream and inclusions, **pre-fat-coat crisped-rice/cocoa crunch**, use **low-moisture/high-fat
    shortcake cookie crumb**, and a color-bleed barrier for red strawberry pieces.
- **Clean-label reality:** the tools that fix high-protein iciness (gums/emulsifiers) are exactly what
  clean-label buyers want gone. A truly zero-gum, 20g, low-sugar bar with great scoop is very hard —
  the honest target is a **short, recognizable label** (milk proteins + inulin + allulose + one
  natural stabilizer if needed), not a stabilizer-free one.

## 4. Regulatory — call it "frozen dairy dessert," and respect the PDCAAS rule

- **Standard of identity (21 CFR 135.110):** "ice cream" requires **≥10% milkfat**, ≥10% nonfat milk
  solids, ≥4.5 lb/gal, ≥1.6 lb solids/gal. A low-fat/high-protein bar almost never clears the 10%
  milkfat floor → label it **"frozen dairy dessert"** (nonstandardized, what Halo Top-style products
  use). You can't say "nonfat ice cream" unless you independently meet 135.110.
- **THE key pitfall — protein claims trigger a PDCAAS-corrected %DV (21 CFR 101.9(c)(7) + 101.54):**
  - Protein **DV = 50g**. "Good source" = **10–19% DV**; "high/excellent" = **≥20% DV** per RACC.
  - Grams on the panel may use the nitrogen (Kjeldahl) method — **but the moment you make _any_
    protein claim** (even "20g protein" front-of-pack), you must declare a **%DV based on the
    PDCAAS-corrected** amount (protein g × PDCAAS ÷ 50g).
  - **Dairy/whey PDCAAS ≈ 1.0**, so 20g dairy protein easily clears "high protein." A plant-protein
    bar with the same grams may **not** qualify — this is precisely the violation behind the **Beyond
    Meat class actions**. Another reason to go dairy.
- **Nutrition Facts:** Added Sugars is mandatory (DV 50g). Sugar-alcohol declaration is voluntary
  _unless_ you make a sugar/polyol claim. **Allulose** (FDA guidance, final 2020): excluded from Total
  & Added Sugars, counted at **0.4 kcal/g**, but kept in Total Carbohydrate and the ingredient list —
  which is why it produces a near-zero-sugar, low-calorie panel. (This is enforcement-discretion
  guidance under active reconsideration — monitor.)
- **Before finalizing label copy:** confirm current eCFR text of 135.110 and 101.9(c)(7) directly and
  validate your specific blend's PDCAAS with a lab.

## 5. Manufacturing & unit economics — co-pack, and mind the whey price

- **Use a co-packer.** Extruded/molded stick novelties **with inclusions + enrobed coating** are a
  specialized subset — many co-packers do pints easily but few do coated bars. Capable names:
  **Perry's** (organic-cert extruded novelty line), **Brothers Desserts**, **Ice Cream Factory**,
  **Gelato NY**. MOQs: standard shapes ~5–10k units; **custom molds 20k+**; meaningful runs are
  hundreds-of-thousands to millions/yr.
- **COGS:** co-packed frozen novelties land **~$0.35–$1.75/unit**; a premium protein bar with clean-
  label ingredients, inclusions, and coating sits at the **top of / above** that (~$1.00–$1.75+/bar
  all-in). **Biggest driver = protein:** WPI hit **~$11/lb (late 2025) → ~$13.50–14/lb (mid-2026)**,
  up 50–110% since 2024 (GLP-1 + protein-craze demand). **Hedge/lock protein pricing.**
- **Pricing & margin stack:** shelf target **$6.99–$8.99 / 4-pack**. Retailer margin on frozen/
  perimeter **~30–40%**; distributor (UNFI/KeHE) headline ~13% but **effective ~20–30%** with fees.
  Wholesale prints COGS at **58–67% of revenue** vs ~26–31% for DTC — a 30–40pt swing to middlemen.
- **Slotting is the hidden line item** — frozen is the most expensive category: small chains up to
  ~$9k; **large-chain frozen $20k–$100k/SKU**; plus free-fill (a case/SKU/store). Reserve capital for
  slotting + trade spend; it often dwarfs other launch costs.
- **Cold chain:** DTC frozen = **$40–$100+/shipment** (dry ice at 1–1.5× product weight + overnight
  freight) with 5–8% spoilage — a margin drain for low-ASP bars. **Retail reefer distribution is the
  scalable path.**
- **Lean-path proof (Halo Top):** bootstrapped from a $20 home machine, ~18 months R&D via Cal Poly's
  Dairy Innovation Institute, ~$500k friends-&-family, near-bankruptcy, then a Jan 2016 viral article
  → >$100M. Lesson: you can start on low-to-mid five figures + a co-packer with tight SKUs, but budget
  **years of formula-durability work** (Halo Top's early product went chalky by shelf) and reserve for
  slotting.

## 6. Go-to-market — natural-channel-first, influencer-led, TikTok-fronted

- **Channel sequence:** launch **natural channel, Sprouts-first** (fast multi-region coverage,
  innovation-friendly buyers) → **Whole Foods** region-by-region → conventional → **club (Costco)** as
  a later scale accelerator. Use **gyms/studios for sampling** (high relevance, small as a sales
  channel). **Amazon/DTC for data & reach, not primary volume** (frozen cold-chain cost).
- **Marketing to "gains":** the proven playbook is **fitness micro/mid influencer seeding + discount
  codes** (drove Halo Top's ~2,500% 2016 jump) with **TikTok as the awareness engine** (demonstrable
  "bars that pull apart" content performs; TikTok Shop food sales doubled YoY). Lead front-of-pack
  with the **bold protein number + low sugar + macro transparency** — now table stakes, not niche.
- **Case-study lessons:** Halo Top — category-defining positioning + digital-first + data-driven
  retail expansion. Yasso — relentless **sampling + repeat-purchase** focus (→15k stores, >$50M,
  Unilever exit). Barebells — modern omni-launch (Vitamin Shoppe / Amazon / DTC / TikTok Shop first,
  then big-box).
- **Shelf reality:** grocery frozen is "pay to play" — budget **slotting + free-fill + UNFI/KeHE fees**
  (KeHE ~18% on free-fill) as a core launch cost. Differentiate on **taste/brand identity** to outlast
  the "protein-maxxing" peak.

## Concrete path to a first production run

1. **Lock the concept:** lead SKU **"Chocolate Crunch" @ 20g protein** (open lane); **"Strawberry
   Shortcake" @ 20g** as the challenger to California Ice's 15g. Frozen dairy dessert, allulose-
   sweetened, ~180–220 cal, low/no added sugar.
2. **Formulate to durability, not just to the line:** MPI/whey-casein base + micellar casein/MPC +
   inulin; allulose + monk fruit; compound coat with a fat barrier + pre-coated crunch. Bench a
   **freeze-thaw abuse test** before committing — this is where protein bars fail.
3. **Get 3 co-packer quotes** for an **extruded, coated bar with inclusions** (Perry's / Brothers /
   Ice Cream Factory), with locked/hedged protein pricing and realistic MOQs.
4. **Nail the label:** "frozen dairy dessert" statement of identity; **PDCAAS-corrected %DV** for the
   protein claim (validate blend PDCAAS with a lab); allulose handled per FDA guidance.
5. **Launch natural-channel-first (Sprouts) + gym sampling + TikTok/fitness-influencer seeding**;
   treat DTC as data/gifting. Budget slotting + free-fill from day one.
6. **Watch the flanks:** David Protein and Barebells entering frozen; whey price volatility; and the
   2026 pivot from "maxxing" to protein _quality_ — build brand equity beyond the spec sheet.
